How to Use the Instructional Designers' Field Guide

By Sara Rose

Step 1: Identify Your Challenge Area

Each section of the Field Guide is organized around common pain points. Use the contents or color-coded tabs to match your situation. You may need to refer to a few areas or follow up online, depending on your needs. But this Field Guide is your best starting point!


Step 2: Flip to the Section that Fits

Once you’ve identified your challenge, go directly to the section that focuses on solving it. Pages are modular — you don’t need to read anything in order. Each section includes clear entry points: checklists, cheat sheets, and step-by-step templates.


Step 3: Use the Tools in Context

Each section provides practical information, tools, best practices and more. This includes checklist, planning worksheets, or sample scripts — to use right away. Tools are available online to print and fill out. Be sure to check back on the website often for updates!


Step 4: Look for the Compass Icon

Sections marked with a small “Compass Icon” contain links to digital tools or updates — including changes to federal standards (e.g., WCAG), AI tools, or evolving best practices. Only sections that benefit from real-time updates include these QR links.

visibilityNote: Some online tools linked in the Field Guide will go live alongside the book release and will not be available for pre-review.


Step 5: Revisit, Reflect, Repeat

This is a living guide. Mark up the margins. Flag pages you use often. Come back when you’re stuck, and use the reflection prompts at the end of each section to track your growth as a designer.

Common Use Cases

  • Onboarding new instructional designers
  • Meeting accessibility compliance during course builds
  • Working with SMEs on tight deadlines
  • Auditing existing courses for gaps
  • Designing training under crisis conditions
